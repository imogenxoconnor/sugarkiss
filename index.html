Our Pūtake & Mission

Freshness over Foliage

Pūtake

Our Pūtake is to prove that self-care does not have to come at the expense of the environment. We provide high-quality beauty essentials that honour the earth through sustainable packaging and zero-waste production methods.

Mission

To empower our community to feel confident and cared for through natural, sustainably packaged beauty products. We are committed to transparency, fair team values, and a made-to-order philosophy that guarantees quality and minimises waste.

Innovation Statement

Sugar Kiss reflects sustainability in the products we distribute. Our innovation is choosing eco-friendly packaging and handcrafting each product so we know every ingredient inside.

Corporate Goals

SMART Goals

1

Sell 120 units of made-to-order, eco-friendly lip scrubs by 30 September through social media marketing and word-of-mouth promotion.

2

Whakaukangia / Sustainability

People, Planet, Culture, and Growth

Tautīnei / Social Sustainability

Sugar Kiss aims to be kind to people and inclusive for everyone. The team makes sure Alyssa, Tayla, and Immy feel respected and have a fair say in how the business runs.

Kaitiakitanga / Environmental Sustainability

We use GreenWay kraft paper jars because they are biodegradable. Made-to-order production helps avoid expired stock and reduces unnecessary waste.

Whai Rawa / Economic Sustainability

Alyssa tracks cost per unit carefully so Sugar Kiss covers ingredients, jars, and shipping while still making a profit to reinvest into better ingredients and new flavours.

Tikanga / Cultural Sustainability

Sugar Kiss aims to feel local to Aotearoa by practising kaitiakitanga, speaking honestly to the community, and respecting New Zealand's diverse cultures.

SWOT Analysis

How Sugar Kiss Competes

Strengths

Sugar Kiss stands out through 100% biodegradable kraft paper jars, fresh handmade production, low-waste operations, and affordable social media marketing that connects with teenagers and young adults.

Weaknesses

Handmade production limits how many units can be made at once. Brand awareness is still new, and the cost per unit may be higher than large companies buying ingredients in bulk.

Opportunities

The natural and sustainable skincare market is growing in New Zealand. Gen Z customers often support small brands that match their values, and social platforms can help Sugar Kiss grow.

Threats

Beauty products are competitive. Bigger brands may adopt sustainable packaging, customers may choose cheaper alternatives, and economic changes can reduce spending on non-essential items.
